TEA@UCSD's 2022 Halloween Maze

About

OPERAHOUSE is Themed Entertainment Association at UCSD's 4th annual Halloween horror maze, in collaboration with Tritonfest. This student designed/run horror maze will be located on campus and free for all UCSD students in Fall 2022. Check out our mazes from previous years:

  • 2021: Wonderland
  • 2019: Petrified
  • 2018: The Last Resort

Get Involved

We are looking for anyone interested in being a part of, or learning about, the design, fabrication, or operation of the maze. There are many open positions for people of all experience levels, if you have any questions feel free to email us at ucsdhauntedmaze@gmail.com.

Committee Leads

We are looking for people to lead our committees! Take a look below for open positions that you may want to be a lead of. If you aren't interested in being a lead, but still want to be a part of the project, you can be a general member and participate in any of the committees listed below.

Open Positions:

The sound designer is responsible for planning the audio in the maze. They are responsible for mixing any required audio. They will determine the speaker layout and co-lead the tech team as a whole.

The projection and video designer is responsible for planning and editing any videos featured in the maze. They will determine the projector placements and video feeds, and co-lead the tech team as a whole.

The music composer is responsible for composing an original soundtrack for the maze. They will work with the Creative Director, Marketing Team, and Sound Designer to recruit musicians and acquire equipment as necessary.

The finance subcommittee lead(s) are responsible for managing the budget of the maze. They are responsible for sending in final orders, updating the state of the budget, and coordinating what has arrived and where it was stored/where to store it. They are NOT responsible for picking out items to purchase, however they do make the final decision based on the budget if a list of potential products to fulfill the same role is sent to them.

The marketing subcommittee lead is in charge of ideating and executing marketing strategies related to the maze, including marketing related to gathering volunteers to be a part of the maze (Scare Actors and Operators). They will manage the mazeâ€™s Instagram Page, but also have the freedom to devise unique marketing strategies. The marketing lead is responsible for ideating the content of advertisements and executing strategies related to marketing (like distributing flyers, spreading facebook posts, or building an installation) but not fabricating the content of the advertisements themselves.

The production design/fabrication lead is responsible for ideating, prototyping, and fabricating the sets (environments) in the maze. They are in charge of identifying the method by which to realize their ideas and finding specific pieces to purchase/generate to execute it. Once materials are acquired they are responsible for assembly/fabrication of the set pieces, and setting it up in its actual location prior to the start of the maze.

The operations lead(s) are in charge of recruiting and managing/training operators. They will work with marketing/graphics to recruit Operators. They will also train operators in their roles, teach them how to use emergency equipment, and how to use walkie talkies. They will work with layout to create the paths and timing of shift changes and facilitate said shift changes on the night of.

The scare acting/operators lead(s) are in charge of recruiting and managing/training scare actors and operators. They will recruit actors and hold auditions to assign roles for scare actors. They will work with scare actors to train them further after auditions to ensure they know both how to play their characters and be effective in a scare maze. Similarly they will train operators in their roles, how to use emergency equipment, and how to use walkie talkies. They will work with layout to create the paths and timing of shift changes and facilitate said shift changes on the night of.

The website lead(s) are responsible for designing and implementing necessary webpages, following software engineering practices. This can range from marketing pages to web apps that augment the maze experience or handle maze logistics (such as virtual queuing/reservations). This committee works closely with the graphics committee for assets and marketing for advertising and recruiting.
